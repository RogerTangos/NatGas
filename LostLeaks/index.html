A Data Story

The Process of How we find Lost Leaks.

[1st]

Get data from the State of MA
Energy and Environmental Affairs

Find records via docket number and download from public utilities The data is usually somewhat irregular, and in pdf format.

[2nd]

Parse PDF's using SmallPDF.com, and PDFTables (Audrey at HEETMA)

Audrey turns the pdfs into excel spreadsheets and cleans the excel data by hand. _link a converted spreadsheet_ (Audrey at HEETMA)

[3rd]

Convert data to CSV, and standardize

Albert Carter converted excel spreadsheets into CSV and ran through python program to standardize and sanitize data. Geolocate using google maps API. Insert into DataHub.

[4th]

Identify Missing Leaks

  • Take list of 2014 Leaks and 2014 Repairs.
  • If a repair happens near an existing leak (approx. 100 ft), count it as repaired.
  • Remaining leaks are counted as un-repaired.
  • If the same leak appears in 2015, it's not missing. Remove it.
  • We know leaks are the same because the date and address are the same.
  • Remaining leaks are missing. Link to the raw JSON file.

have someone put in their address and look for leaks around their address have someone perform a select query for leaks older than 5 years
